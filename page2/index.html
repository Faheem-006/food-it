Ingredients

Instructions


For the Spaghetti Pasta


Spaghetti noodles


Water for boiling


Salt (for seasoning the pasta water)


Olive oil (optional, for preventing the noodles from sticking)


Tomato sauce or marinara sauce (for serving)


Grated Parmesan cheese (for garnish)


Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish, optional)


Ground beef or Italian sausage (optional, for adding protein)


Additional vegetables like onions, bell peppers,
or mushrooms (optional, for added flavor and nutrition)


Boil Water: Fill a large pot with water, adding a generous amount of salt. Bring the water to a rolling boil over high heat.

Add Spaghetti: Once the water is boiling, carefully add the spaghetti noodles to the pot. Use a spoon to gently push the noodles into the water to ensure they are fully submerged.

Cook Pasta: Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions, typically around 8-10 minutes for al dente pasta. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

Test for Doneness: After the recommended cooking time, use tongs or a fork to remove a strand of spaghetti from the pot. Taste it to check for doneness. It should be tender but still slightly firm (al dente).

Drain Pasta: Once the spaghetti is cooked to your desired texture, turn off the heat and carefully drain the pasta in a colander placed in the sink. Shake the colander gently to remove excess water.

Rinse (Optional): Some people prefer to rinse the spaghetti under cold water to stop the cooking process and prevent it from sticking together. This step is optional and depends on personal preference.

Serve: Transfer the drained spaghetti to a serving dish or individual plates. Top with your favorite sauce, such as tomato sauce or marinara, and any desired toppings like grated Parmesan cheese, fresh herbs, or cooked meatballs.

Enjoy: Serve the spaghetti immediately while it's hot and enjoy your delicious meal!
